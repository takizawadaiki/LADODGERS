Baseline (2022–23): Dodgers were already elite — $4.8B valuation, back-to-back NL West titles, strong local TV revenue. But international presence was limited, social growth had plateaued, and there were fewer than 5 Japanese brand sponsors.
Year 1 (2024): Ohtani won NL MVP and became the first player in baseball history to hit 50 HR and steal 50 bases in one season. Dodgers won the World Series. Revenue crossed $1 billion — an MLB first. $70M in new Japanese sponsorships. Jersey sales broke Fanatics' all-time record within 48 hours.
Today (2025–26): Back-to-back World Series champions. First team to draw 4 million fans since 2008. Franchise value at $7.8B. 20 Japanese brand sponsors. Ohtani returned to two-way duties and is projected to earn $127M in 2026 from salary and endorsements combined.
Projected (2027–33): Mid-contract prime years for both players. Deferred payments on Ohtani's deal don't start until 2034, keeping current cash outlay minimal. Continued franchise value appreciation, growing Japan market, and sustained on-field contention expected.

Verdict: the ~$1B spend is paying off — by a large margin

The contracts are brilliantly structured. Ohtani collects just $2M per year through 2033 — back-loaded to $68M annually starting 2034 — making this effectively an interest-free loan to the franchise during his peak playing years.

Year 1 alone returned $70M in new Japanese sponsorships, pushed total revenue past $1 billion (an MLB first), and delivered a World Series championship. Franchise value has grown by ~$3 billion since the signings — roughly 3× the present value of both contracts combined (~$462M NPV for Ohtani alone at 3% inflation discount).

Social media, Google search, and Japan viewership data confirm this isn't just domestic hype: the Dodgers have built a genuinely global audience. The sponsorship data alone — from 4 Japanese brands pre-signing to 20 today — shows the commercial infrastructure now in place.

The Ohtani and Yamamoto signings are already among the highest-ROI transactions in sports business history.
